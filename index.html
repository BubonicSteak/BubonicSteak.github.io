Rules

Competition Rules

  1. All fish must be caught in Indiana and/or states touching Indiana. This includes Indiana, Michigan, Illinois, Kentucky, and Ohio.
  2. The largest fish per species will be determined by the fish’s length and/or a large difference is noticed between a fish’s weight (5lbs or more). For example, if the current biggest fish is 20in 2 lbs. and a fish that is 19in 7 lbs., the fish with 19in 7 lbs. will become the largest fish.
  3. All fish species recorded must be on the excel sheet. Identification must be confirmed between the participants.
  4. Only fish photographed properly will be added. Any fish not photographed will not be counted. For size, the length and weight of the fish must be properly photographed.
  5. The competition spans each fishing license duration. This means that when your license for the season expires the competition ends.
  6. In the event of a tie in total points the person with the largest fish will break the tie.
  7. In the event of a fish that is not on the excel sheet is caught, the fish could be added to the sheet upon reviewal of all participants.

Format

  1. All new species account for a point toward your total.
  2. The largest of a big game species will give you an extra 2 points. This point will be taken away in the event that a larger fish is caught.
  3. Rare fish (the fish in red) are worth 5 points if recorded. If multiple big game rare fish are recorded the largest of the entries will get an additional 3 points.
  4. Extremely rare fish(Lake Sturgeon) Are worth 20 points with additional 6 points for the largest if multiple entries are recorded.
  5. The person who accumulates the most points will win the competition.
  6. Prizes are undetermined.
